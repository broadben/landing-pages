FAQ

Common questions.

PropPull works on the public-facing property pages of Zillow, Redfin, and Realtor.com. MLS data behind agent logins isn't currently supported — but for most research workflows, these sources cover everything you need.
Tax data (annual taxes, assessed value) is scraped from property pages on Zillow and Redfin when they display it. Direct county assessor scraping is on the roadmap.
PropPull connects to your existing DocuSign account via OAuth. When you click "Send via DocuSign," it creates a formatted property summary document and sends it as an envelope to whoever you specify. You'll need a DocuSign account (free developer accounts are available).
All scraped property data is stored locally in your browser's storage — never on our servers. The only external calls are when you explicitly export to Google Sheets (your webhook) or send via DocuSign.
PropPull uses multiple fallback selectors for each field, so minor DOM changes won't break it. If Zillow does a major redesign, we push an update to the Chrome Web Store — typically within 48 hours.
PropPull is optimized for residential properties today. Commercial support (LoopNet, Crexi) is on the roadmap. If that's important to you, let us know — it helps us prioritize.
